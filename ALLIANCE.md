# Open Growing Alliance: Organizational Manifesto

## About the Alliance

The **Open Growing Alliance** is a community-driven organization stewarding the [Open Growing Protocol (OGP)](profile/README.md)—an open protocol for controlled environment agriculture, starting with home cannabis cultivation.

We are not the protocol. We are its caretakers.

The Alliance exists to:
- Maintain and evolve OGP specifications through transparent community governance
- Foster a collaborative ecosystem of growers, developers, and researchers
- Ensure the protocol remains permanently open source and vendor-neutral
- Coordinate community efforts while preventing capture by any single entity
- Provide infrastructure for collaboration (repositories, discussions, documentation)

### Where We Are Today (Early 2026)

We're **early stage**—small team, focused scope, building foundations. We're not pretending to be an established organization with formal structures we don't yet have.

Current reality:
- **Organization**: Founder-led with early contributors, moving toward community governance
- **Protocol**: OGP v2.0 specification complete, reference implementations in progress
- **Community**: Growing slowly through authentic engagement, not marketing hype
- **Focus**: Cannabis CEA for home growers—proven domain before expansion

We're sharing this openly because honest communication builds trust. We're building the Alliance we want to see, not the one we wish we already were.

## Our Role as Protocol Stewards

The Alliance maintains OGP, but **does not own it**. This distinction matters:

- **OGP is a public good**: Once published, specifications are part of the commons
- **The Alliance can disappear**: The protocol survives if implementations exist
- **Multiple stewards are welcome**: If others want to maintain OGP, that strengthens resilience
- **Community trumps organization**: If the Alliance fails to serve growers, the community should fork

We're stewards, not gatekeepers. Our authority comes from service, not control.

## Governance

### Current Structure (Transparent About Early Stage)

Today, governance is **pragmatic and founder-led** while building toward community structures:

- **Decision-making**: Core team discusses proposals in public GitHub discussions
- **Technical changes**: Specification changes require public RFCs with community input
- **Transparency**: All decisions documented, reasoning explained, alternatives considered
- **Accountability**: Community can fork, criticize, or propose alternatives at any time

### Evolution Toward Community Governance

As the community grows, governance will evolve:

- **Working Groups**: Domain experts (growers, hardware, protocol spec) guide their areas
- **RFC Process**: Formal process for protocol changes with clear acceptance criteria
- **Consensus Building**: Major decisions require broad community support, not just core team approval
- **Conflict Resolution**: Transparent processes for disputes, with community input

We're not imposing elaborate governance prematurely. Structures emerge from actual needs, not abstract ideals.

### What We're Committed To

No matter how governance evolves:
- **Public decision-making**: No backroom deals, no hidden agendas
- **Vendor neutrality**: No single company or commercial interest controls the protocol
- **Open source forever**: Permanent commitment to open licensing (Apache 2.0, CC BY 4.0)
- **Community veto**: If the Alliance acts against grower interests, the community can fork

## Membership & Participation

### Who Can Participate

**Everyone.** No barriers, no gatekeeping.

You are part of the Alliance if you:
- Use OGP in your growing
- Contribute to specifications or implementations
- Participate in discussions
- Share growing recipes or techniques
- Test protocol features and report results
- Document your setup or learnings
- Ask questions or provide feedback

There are no membership fees, no applications, no approval processes. Participation is contribution.

### Types of Contribution

The Alliance needs diverse skills and perspectives:

**Growers**: Your domain expertise validates everything we build. Share techniques, test implementations, document real-world challenges.

**Developers**: Build reference implementations, integrations, tools. Make OGP accessible to non-technical growers.

**Researchers**: Run experiments, analyze data, validate claims. Bring scientific rigor to cultivation techniques.

**Technical Writers**: Translate specifications into practical guidance. Make complex protocols understandable.

**Organizers**: Facilitate discussions, coordinate working groups, maintain community infrastructure.

**Advocates**: Share OGP with other growers, explain benefits, build awareness through authentic engagement.

All roles matter. A grower documenting a single successful recipe contributes as meaningfully as a developer writing reference implementations.

### Recognition & Attribution

Contributors deserve credit:
- Recipe creators are attributed in shared growing techniques
- Code contributors are acknowledged in implementation repositories
- Specification contributors are listed in version histories
- Community members who help others are recognized in discussions

We value reputation earned through contribution, not titles granted by hierarchy.

## Alliance Values

### 1. Community Over Corporate

We exist to serve **home growers**, not investor returns.

This means:
- Protocol decisions prioritize grower autonomy over commercial convenience
- We accept slower growth to maintain community trust
- We refuse funding that creates conflicts of interest
- We build for actual needs, not hypothetical markets

If the Alliance ever contradicts these principles, the community should hold us accountable.

### 2. Radically Open

**Everything public. Everything transparent.**

- All specifications: Open source (Apache 2.0, CC BY 4.0)
- All discussions: Public forums, documented decisions
- All governance: Community participation, transparent processes
- All failures: Honest acknowledgment, public post-mortems

Openness creates accountability. Secrecy serves power, not community.

### 3. Honest About Scope & Limits

We build tools for **personal cultivation sovereignty in controlled environment cannabis growing**. We do not:
- Promise to solve industrial agriculture's problems
- Claim to democratize food systems (systemic inequities require systemic solutions)
- Pretend cannabis techniques automatically transfer to all crops
- Overpromise capabilities we haven't proven

We succeed by delivering real value in a focused domain, not by making grandiose claims.

### 4. Solarpunk Pragmatism

We're guided by solarpunk principles—technology for human flourishing, local resilience, ecological balance—but we're pragmatic about implementation:

- **Appropriate technology**: Simple tools that work beat complex promises
- **Progressive automation**: Start manual, add capability as growers learn
- **Energy honesty**: CEA consumes resources—we measure and optimize, not greenwash
- **Local-first design**: Systems work independently, sync when convenient

Ideals guide, reality constrains, practicality wins.

### 5. Attribution & Commons

**Knowledge is commons. Contributors deserve recognition.**

- Growers who develop techniques get credit when others use their recipes
- Developers who build implementations are acknowledged
- Researchers who validate methods are cited
- Community members who help others are visible

Sharing doesn't require anonymity. Attribution builds trust and encourages contribution.

## Code of Conduct

### Expected Behavior

Be respectful, inclusive, and collaborative:
- **Assume good intentions**: Most conflicts stem from misunderstanding, not malice
- **Critique ideas, not people**: Disagree about technical choices without personal attacks
- **Welcome newcomers**: Everyone was a beginner once
- **Share knowledge generously**: Help others learn without condescension
- **Acknowledge uncertainty**: "I don't know" is better than confidently wrong

### Unacceptable Behavior

We will not tolerate:
- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary behavior
- Personal attacks or inflammatory language
- Posting others' private information without consent
- Commercial spam or undisclosed conflicts of interest
- Deliberate disruption of community spaces

### Enforcement

Violations will be addressed:
1. **Warning**: First incidents receive private feedback about impact
2. **Temporary ban**: Repeated violations result in time-limited removal from community spaces
3. **Permanent ban**: Serious violations or continued patterns result in permanent removal

The community deserves safe, productive spaces. We enforce boundaries to protect that.

## Our Relationship to OGP

Understanding the Alliance's relationship to the protocol:

### The Alliance Maintains OGP

We coordinate:
- Specification development and versioning
- Reference implementation repositories
- Community discussions about protocol evolution
- Documentation and learning resources

### The Protocol Can Outlive the Alliance

OGP exists independently of this organization:
- Specifications are permanently open source
- Implementations belong to their creators
- If the Alliance dissolves, the protocol persists
- Other organizations can maintain OGP alongside or instead of us

### The Community Owns the Future

The Alliance serves the community, not vice versa:
- If we fail to serve growers' interests, fork us
- If we become captured by commercial interests, replace us
- If we make bad technical decisions, override us

We succeed only when the community thrives. The protocol matters more than the organization.

## How to Get Involved

### 1. Lurk & Learn

No pressure to jump in immediately:
- Read the [OGP Manifesto](profile/README.md) to understand protocol vision
- Browse [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/orgs/opengrowingalliance/discussions) to see current conversations
- Review [specifications](https://github.com/opengrowingalliance/specifications) to understand technical details
- Watch repositories to follow development progress

Take your time. Understand before contributing.

### 2. Introduce Yourself

When ready:
- Share your background (grower, developer, researcher, curious observer)
- Describe your interest in OGP (solving specific problems, general learning, potential contribution)
- Ask questions about anything confusing
- Connect with others working on similar challenges

No formal introduction required—jump into discussions when comfortable.

### 3. Start Small

First contributions should be low-risk:
- **For growers**: Test a simple recipe, document results
- **For developers**: Fix documentation typos, improve error messages
- **For researchers**: Validate protocol claims, identify gaps
- **For writers**: Clarify confusing explanations, add examples

Small contributions build familiarity. Sustainable engagement beats heroic sprints.

### 4. Find Your Niche

As you understand the ecosystem:
- Identify areas matching your skills and interests
- Notice gaps where you could add value
- Connect with working groups aligned to your expertise
- Propose projects you want to tackle

Contribution should align with your strengths and curiosities.

### 5. Coordinate Before Big Efforts

Before investing significant time:
- Discuss your proposal in relevant discussions or issues
- Get feedback on approach and scope
- Ensure it aligns with protocol direction
- Find collaborators or reviewers

Coordination prevents duplicated effort and misaligned work.

## Our Promise to the Community

The Open Growing Alliance commits to:

✓ **Permanent open source** - OGP specifications and reference implementations remain freely available

✓ **Transparent governance** - All decisions public, all processes documented

✓ **Community accountability** - Serve growers' interests, not commercial capture

✓ **Honest communication** - Acknowledge limits, admit mistakes, avoid overpromising

✓ **Vendor neutrality** - No single commercial interest controls the protocol

✓ **Inclusive participation** - No barriers to contribution beyond basic respect

✓ **Attribution & recognition** - Contributors get credit for their work

✓ **Protocol stewardship** - Maintain OGP as a public good, not organizational property

If we fail to uphold these commitments, hold us accountable. Fork us. Replace us. Build the Alliance the community deserves.

We are here to serve growers building personal cultivation sovereignty, one tent at a time.
