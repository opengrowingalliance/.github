# The OGP Manifesto: Growing Personal Sovereignty, One Garden at a Time

## A Call to Reclaim Control

In a world where growing technology increasingly serves corporate interests over personal autonomy, where cultivation knowledge is locked behind proprietary systems, and where growers must choose between expensive turnkey solutions and DIY complexity, we're building a different path.

The Open Growing Protocol (OGP) is a technical foundation for **personal cultivation sovereignty**—starting with controlled environment agriculture for home cannabis growers, guided by solarpunk principles, with a vision to expand mindfully.

This is not a universal solution to agriculture's challenges. This is a focused effort to prove that open protocols, appropriate technology, and community-driven development can work—one basement, one tent, one grower at a time.

## What OGP Enables Today

OGP creates concrete, practical tools for home growers:

- **A home grower in their basement** can automate their cannabis tent using affordable sensors and open-source controllers, avoiding vendor lock-in
- **A cultivator perfecting their technique** can share their exact growing recipe with someone across the country, with the system automatically adapting to different hardware configurations
- **A hobbyist starting with basic equipment** can begin with manual cultivation guided by recipes, then progressively add automation as they learn and invest
- **A network of home growers** can collectively improve cultivation techniques through shared data and experiments, with proper attribution for each contributor's insights

These capabilities are built on OGP's technical foundations:

- **Recipe Portability**: Define growing recipes once, deploy across different hardware, with automatic adaptation
- **Hardware Interoperability**: Mix sensors and actuators from different manufacturers, breaking vendor lock-in
- **Progressive Automation**: Start manual with recipe guidance, add automation incrementally as resources allow
- **Knowledge Commons**: Share cultivation methodologies in standardized formats with proper attribution
- **Appropriate Technology**: Run on affordable hardware (Raspberry Pi to industrial controllers) with graceful degradation
- **Community Governance**: Transparent, inclusive decision-making that prevents capture by any single entity

## Our Solarpunk Principles

### 1. Personal Sovereignty Through Open Technology

We believe growers should own their data, choose their hardware, and control their automation without depending on corporate platforms. Technology should distribute power, not concentrate it.

**What this means**: We're building for home growers who want independence from proprietary systems, not trying to disrupt industrial agriculture. We're starting focused because authentic sovereignty requires proven tools, not grand promises.

**Contribute**: Help develop low-cost sensor alternatives, test OGP on affordable hardware, or document DIY approaches that lower barriers to entry.

### 2. Knowledge as Commons, Not Commodity

Cultivation knowledge—especially techniques developed by specialized communities like cannabis growers—should be shared freely with proper attribution. Knowledge commons thrive when contributors are recognized and trust is maintained.

**What this means**: We're creating systems for growers to share techniques while getting credit for their work. We're not claiming to preserve indigenous knowledge (that requires co-design with those communities) or democratize all agriculture (that requires addressing systemic inequities beyond our scope).

**Contribute**: Document your growing techniques in OGP recipe formats, help develop attribution systems that respect contributors, or share experimental results with the community.

### 3. Appropriate Technology Over Feature Bloat

We prioritize solutions matched to context. A Raspberry Pi running proven automation beats vaporware promises of AI-powered universal farming systems. Simple, working tools that growers can understand and modify beat complex black boxes.

**What this means**: We're building for today's home growers with today's affordable hardware, not promising future miracles. Progressive automation means starting simple and adding capability as growers gain experience and resources.

**Contribute**: Develop implementations for single-board computers, create clear documentation for DIY builders, or design interfaces that work for growers with varying technical skills.

### 4. Local Resilience Through Decentralized Systems

Centralized platforms create dependencies and single points of failure. Local-first systems that can operate independently and sync when convenient create resilience and maintain grower autonomy.

**What this means**: We're designing for home growers who want systems that work even when internet connections fail, not building cloud platforms that own user data. Decentralization serves personal control, not techno-libertarian ideology.

**Contribute**: Develop offline-capable interfaces, create local synchronization mechanisms, or build mesh networking capabilities for multi-tent operations.

### 5. Honest Sustainability Through Measured Impact

We track energy use, measure resource efficiency, and acknowledge tradeoffs. Controlled environment agriculture consumes energy—the question is whether we can optimize it and integrate renewables, not whether we can eliminate physics.

**What this means**: We're building tools to help growers understand and reduce their environmental impact, not claiming to solve climate change through home cultivation. Sustainability requires transparency about resource use, not greenwashing.

**Contribute**: Develop energy monitoring features, create dashboards showing resource efficiency, or design integrations with renewable energy systems.

## Who We Need

### Developers and Makers

If you build hardware or write code, we need your skills to create tools that work reliably for home growers. We're focused on practical implementations—Raspberry Pi controllers, sensor integrations, user interfaces that don't require engineering degrees.

**What we need**: Reference implementations for common hardware, interfaces designed for growers (not developers), documentation that assumes curiosity but not expertise, and testing on real growing setups.

### Cannabis Cultivators and Home Growers

Your knowledge and experience validate everything we build. Whether you're perfecting strains in a tent, experimenting with hydroponics, or running multiple grows, your insights drive OGP's development.

**What we need**: Test early implementations and provide honest feedback, document your techniques in formats that preserve your attribution, share what you actually need from automation (not what vendors think you need), and help us understand the real pain points of home cultivation.

### Technical Writers and Educators

Clear documentation and accessible learning resources make the difference between a protocol that serves developers and one that serves growers. We need people who can translate between technical specifications and practical growing guidance.

**What we need**: User-friendly guides for setting up OGP systems, tutorials that assume no prior programming experience, recipe documentation that captures both the "what" and the "why", and feedback on where our technical communication fails actual users.

### Researchers and Experimentalists

Methodical testing and data-driven validation help us improve growing techniques and protocol reliability. If you approach cultivation scientifically or love controlled experiments, your rigor strengthens the community.

**What we need**: Controlled experiments comparing growing methodologies, analysis of environmental data to validate recipe claims, testing of protocol implementations under varied conditions, and honest reporting of what works and what doesn't.

## Getting Started

Ready to contribute? Here are concrete first steps:

1. **Explore the Protocol**: Read the [OGSP specification](https://github.com/opengrowingalliance/specifications/blob/main/v2.0/ogsp-root-spec.md) to understand current capabilities and scope
2. **Join Discussions**: Introduce yourself in [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/orgs/opengrowingalliance/discussions) and connect with others working on similar challenges
3. **Start Practical**: Try implementing a simple cannabis recipe for a grow you're already running—learn by doing with real plants
4. **Share Honestly**: Document what works, what doesn't, and what you learned. Failure reports help the community as much as success stories
5. **Find Your Contribution**: Pick projects that match your current skills and available time—impact comes from sustained small contributions, not occasional heroics

### Beginner-Friendly Contributions

- **Recipe Testing**: Run existing OGP recipes in your grow and document results (especially deviations and problems)
- **Hardware Documentation**: Document how you connected sensors to your controller, with photos and wiring diagrams
- **Pain Point Mapping**: Tell us where the protocol, docs, or tools confused you—fresh eyes catch what developers miss
- **Use Case Validation**: Help us understand if OGP actually solves your growing automation needs or if we're building the wrong thing
- **Small Code Contributions**: Fix typos, improve error messages, add validation—not everything requires architectural knowledge

## The Path Forward

OGP is a **starting point**, not a finished solution. We're building tools for personal cultivation sovereignty in cannabis CEA because it's a domain we understand, with growers who need open alternatives, and constraints that force us to solve real problems.

If this works—if we can prove that open protocols, appropriate technology, and community governance can create genuine value for home growers—then we'll have a foundation to expand from. Not a blueprint to copy-paste onto other domains, but validated principles and battle-tested patterns.

**Our vision**: Personal sovereignty in controlled environment cultivation, starting with cannabis home growing, expanding mindfully as we learn what actually works and where our approach genuinely adds value.

**Our commitment**: We will not promise what we cannot deliver. We will acknowledge our scope limits. We will measure our impact honestly. We will remain accountable to growers, not growth metrics.

**Our invitation**: Join us in building something real. Not saving agriculture, not disrupting food systems, not solving climate change—just creating tools that give home growers genuine control over their cultivation, one tent at a time.

If that resonates with you, we'd be honored to grow alongside you.
